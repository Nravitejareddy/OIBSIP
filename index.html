Albert Einstein

The mind that redefined the universe

Biography

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist whose ideas transformed humanity’s understanding of space, time, energy, and the universe.

A philosopher and humanist, he believed science must serve humanity and peace. His work bridged imagination and logic, changing modern civilization.

His theories of relativity, contributions to quantum mechanics, and the equation E = mc² made him the most iconic scientist in history.

Publications & Thought

“Only a life lived for others is a life worthwhile.”

Einstein’s publications revolutionized physics. His 1905 papers introduced special relativity and the photoelectric effect, while his 1915 work on general relativity redefined gravity.

Many of his ideas were confirmed decades later, proving the power of deep thought experiments guided by mathematical precision.

Elevator Experiment

Einstein imagined a person inside a falling elevator and realized that gravity and acceleration feel identical, forming the Equivalence Principle, the foundation of General Relativity.

Chasing a Light Beam

By imagining chasing a beam of light, he concluded that light speed is constant, forcing time and space to become relative — the birth of Special Relativity.

Brownian Motion

Einstein explained the random motion of particles in fluids, providing proof of atoms and influencing the development of statistical mechanics and quantum theory.

Einstein & Planck

Max Planck was among the first to recognize Einstein’s genius. Their ideas jointly shaped the foundations of quantum physics.

Despite contrasting personalities, they shared music, friendship, and a passion for fundamental truth.

Awards & Honors

Einstein received the Nobel Prize for explaining the photoelectric effect, a cornerstone of modern quantum mechanics.

The element Einsteinium was named in his honor, symbolizing his timeless scientific legacy.
